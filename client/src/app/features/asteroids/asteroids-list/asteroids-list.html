Near-Earth objects are asteroids and comets whose orbits bring them close to our planet. NASA tracks thousands of them; this view lists those making a close approach over the next seven days.

“Potentially hazardous” is a formal classification for larger objects passing within about 7.5 million km — flagged for monitoring, not an imminent threat. Data from NASA's NeoWs.
